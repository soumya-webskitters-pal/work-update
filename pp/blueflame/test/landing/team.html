Raj Bakhru, CISSP, CFA

Chief Executive Officer

Raj draws on a wide-ranging background encompassing sales, marketing, software development, corporate growth, and business management. Throughout his career, he has played a central role in developing top-tier tools in alternative investments and cybersecurity.

Formerly Chief Strategy Officer at ACA, Raj oversaw corporate development and M&A, also serving as Interim Co-CEO, Chief Innovation Officer, and Head of RegTech and ESG. He was the founder of Aponix, later ACA's cyber division, a leader in the alternatives sector. Raj's experience includes roles as a quantitative software developer at Kepos Capital, Highbridge, and Goldman Sachs Asset Management. He holds a B.S. in Computer Engineering from Columbia University, along with CISSP and CFA credentials.

Christopher Cox

Former SEC Chairman

Chris Cox is recently retired as a partner in the international law firm of Morgan, Lewis & Bockius LLP and as president of Morgan Lewis Consulting LLC, the firm’s global consulting arm. Over the course of twenty years in the private practice of law, he was a partner in the international law firms of Bingham McCutchen LLP and Latham & Watkins LLP. He also taught federal income tax as a member of the faculty at Harvard Business School.

During a twenty-three year Washington career, he was a White House counsel to President Ronald Reagan, chairman of the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission, chairman of the Homeland Security Committee in the U.S. House of Representatives, and the fifth-ranking elected leader in the House.

He currently serves as a member of the boards of directors of real estate, sports, and consumer products investor Revitate LLC and technology industry association NetChoice, Inc., and as a Life Trustee of the University of Southern California. Prior boards include ophthalmic lens manufacturer RxSight, Inc.; governance, risk and compliance services provider ACA Group; private equity and investment advisor Starr Investment Holdings; global photonics leader Newport Corporation; health care products and services company Alphaeon (now Evolus and Alphaeon Credit); and governance, risk and compliance firm Thomson Reuters Accelus.

He holds a B.A. from the University of Southern California, M.B.A. from Harvard Business School, J.D. from Harvard Law School, where he was as an Editor of the Harvard Law Review, and honorary Ph.D. from Northeastern University.
